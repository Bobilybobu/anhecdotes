HOW IT BEGAN

It all began with a simple, playful zombie-shooting Scratch game made by a curious 6th grader. Mesmerized by the idea of playing my own creation—and even being able to "hack" it—I embarked on my coding journey. Little did I know that a few years later, I would be coding robots and working with university students in one of Hanoi’s leading tech universities.

The experience has been nothing short of incredible, full of surprises at every turn. And yet, this is just the beginning of my engineering journey.

DUAL-ARM YUMI ROBOT

This is ABB’s Dual-arm YuMi collaborative robot. It features two articulated arms that can work together seamlessly, allowing for intricate tasks such as assembly, packaging, and handling delicate components. Each arm has up to seven degrees of freedom, enabling the robotic arms to perform complex tasks with high precision and dexterity, similar to the movements of a human arm.

It comes with a pendant that can control the robotic arm through a simple joystick. Additionally, the robot includes a unique function that allows users to manually position the arm at specific points, effectively “teaching” it those locations. This capability makes it easy to obtain the coordinates needed for programming tasks, streamlining the setup process and enhancing usability.



AUTOMATED SORTING SYSTEM



My proudest achievement is my creation of a RAPID-based program for ABB’s Dual-arm YuMi collaborative robot which automatically sorts tomatoes based on ripeness. This accomplishment isn’t an overnight effort; it’s the culmination of months of intensive learning and trial.

Unlike the games and websites I’d programmed before where problems had straightforward solutions, the real world presents uncontrollable factors that require a more innovative approach. Through countless trials, I improved the robot’s ability to grasp differently-shaped tomatoes and the accuracy of the robot’s judgments.

Through this project, I aim to use the knowledge gained during my time as a student to approach global technology trends. Several development directions could make the product sorting and automated storage system more efficient and better meet the needs of businesses and the market. AI and machine learning can be applied to predict storage demand and consumer trends, optimizing the arrangement and storage of goods. Leveraging the system's self-learning capabilities can continuously improve performance and adaptability over time. The automated storage system can also be integrated with order and delivery processes, reducing time and costs related to transportation.

In conclusion, the project achieved several successes, such as designing a model of an automated product sorting and storage system using the Yumi robot to move products, and applying IoT technology for remote control and monitoring of the system. A real product model was constructed, programmed for control, and monitored to ensure stable and accurate operation.


VIDEOS

After finishing the basics, I moved on to making it hold a marker, allowing it to draw shapes with precise control and accuracy.
The coordinates were determined through manual jogging and recorded by hand. Though no coding was involved, accurately positioning the arm was a meticulous challenge, enhancing my technical skills.
By obtaining the points, I could make the robotic arm draw any object I wanted. In this video, I tried to draw a heart by making each arm draw half.
To capture the movement of the robotic arm, I attached LED lights to its grippers and used a slow shutter speed to create a long-exposure photograph, revealing the path it traced in a single, fluid image.
The result was a beautifully crafted heart, formed with precise straight lines and graceful arcs
